The Setting

Untouched Waters at the
Edge of the World

Deep in Northern Patagonia, far from the tourist corridors of Bariloche and San Martín, the Alumine River carves through ancient volcanic landscapes rarely seen by outsiders. Bravo Sur Lodge has quietly guarded access to these waters since 1998.

We are the only fly fishing lodge on the entire river. That means no competition for runs, no crowded banks, no pressure on fish. Just you, your guide, and thousands of wild brown and rainbow trout that have never seen an artificial fly from another operation.

We float remote canyon stretches by drift boat, wade crystal tributaries in the upper reaches, and spend lake days targeting trophy fish in snowmelt waters. Every day is adapted in real time to conditions — because that's what world-class guiding looks like.

The Fishing

Trophy Trout in Water Built for Big Fish

Target Species #1

Brown Trout

The crown jewel of Patagonian fly fishing. Migratory fish entering the Alumine during their spawning run grow to extraordinary sizes — some of the largest wild freshwater brown trout anywhere on the planet.

Target Species #2

Rainbow Trout

Acrobatic, powerful, and spectacular fighters. Patagonian rainbows are built differently — longer runs, bigger jumps, more attitude. On streamer or dry fly, they'll test every knot in your leader.

Drift Boat

Float prime canyon stretches with your guide rowing to position. Access runs impossible to reach on foot — the most productive approach for big migratory fish in mid-river holding lies.

Wading

Step into the upper Alumine and its wild tributaries. Classic pocket water with beautiful riffles — the purest form of Patagonian fly fishing, tight to the river and completely immersed.

Lake Fishing

Optional days on remote Pulmari and Quillén Lakes target cruising fish in crystal-clear snowmelt water. Shore casting and drift boat fishing for browns and rainbows pushing into the shallows.

Sample Itinerary

8 Days · 7 Nights on the Alumine

Adapted daily to real river conditions, water levels, and seasonal hatches

Day 1

Arrival — Chapelco Airport (CPC)

Our team meets you in San Martín de los Andes for the scenic transfer to Bravo Sur Lodge. Welcome dinner and evening program briefing with your lead guide.

Day 2

Abra Ancha — Avispero · Upper Alumine

Float the pristine upper stretch. Classic pocket water with beautiful riffles — ideal for browns and rainbows on streamers and dry flies. Gourmet riverside lunch.

Day 3

Avispero — Puesto Quemado · Mid Alumine

Stunning canyon stretches with productive riffles and deep pools. Some of the most beautiful — and most productive — water in the entire program.

Day 4

Puesto Quemado — Lodge · Trophy Section

The marquee float. Riffles, undercut banks, and willow-lined stretches in the heart of big-fish water. We drift back to the lodge as the light fades on the canyon.

Day 5

Remote Wilderness Waters · Camping Camilo

Boat access to extremely remote sections with near-zero fishing pressure. Exceptional fish holding in deep, undisturbed pools — the kind of water that resets your expectations.

Day 6

Playa de Piedra — Pilo Lil · Lower Alumine

The lower section offers dramatic Rocky landscapes, faster currents, and technical water that rewards precise presentations. A different dimension of the river's character.

Day 7

Pulmari Lake · Optional Lake Day

Shore casting and drift boat fishing in crystal-clear snowmelt water for cruising rainbow and brown trout. A spectacular change of pace after days on the river.

Day 8

Departure — Until Next Season

Breakfast at the lodge. Scenic transfer back to Chapelco Airport. Pack your stories carefully — they'll fish well back home.

Season & Availability

A Short Window.
A Life-Long Memory.

The Patagonian fly fishing season runs from November through April — six months of prime water conditions in the Southern Hemisphere's summer. Our program operates across the full season, with different techniques and target species as the river evolves.

The most coveted weeks are the big-fish months of April and May, when large migratory brown trout push through the system. These dates are typically the first to be reserved.

We host a limited number of anglers per season to protect the fishing quality and maintain our service standards. Once the program is full, we do not add additional weeks. If you're considering a trip, early planning is essential.
